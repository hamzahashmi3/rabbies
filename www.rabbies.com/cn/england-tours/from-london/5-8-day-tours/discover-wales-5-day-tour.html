        浪迹威尔士5日游

        探索三个国家公园， 参观令人叹为观止的城堡，了解古代凯尔特文化，这将会是一趟去往威尔士的终极之旅。

        您将游览的景点


        埃文河畔斯特拉特福 (Stratford-upon-Avon) - 古老的木房子使这座小镇成为历史爱好者的天堂，大文豪威廉•莎士比亚的故居就在这座小镇上。

        威尔士无主之地和寒武纪山脉 (Welsh Borderlands and the Cambrian Mountains) - 这片温和而绵延起伏的丘陵分裂了大不列颠王国数百年。

        斯诺登尼亚国家公园 (Snowdonia National Park) - 威尔士第一个国家公园，位于威尔士西北部， 是除了湖区以外英国人最喜爱的国家公园，拥有威尔士最高峰斯诺登山。

        哈莱克城堡 (Harlech Castle) - 始建于13世纪，当时是作为英格兰国王爱德华一世远征威尔士的据点而建设。联合国教科文组织描述这座城堡为“欧洲13世纪晚期军事建筑的最好的例子”。

        新码头和卡迪根湾 (New Quay and Cardigan Bay) - 这里是爱尔兰海的一片海域，位于威尔士西部 ，卡迪根湾拥有众多的海滩。这是一片古雅华丽的区域，不远处的山上有典雅的白色房子，从这里可以俯瞰整片海滩。

        多勒柯提金矿 (Dolaucothi Gold Mine) - 参观古罗马在英国留下的唯一金矿。

        狄兰·托马斯的拉恩镇 (Laugharne and its Dylan Thomas connections) - 探索启发了世界上最伟大的诗人之一狄兰·托马斯的城镇。

        彭布罗克郡海岸国家公园 和普雷瑟里山(Pembrokeshire Coast National Park & the Preseli Mountains) - 英国唯一一处因拥有雄壮海岸线而设立的国家公园壮观的海岸，岸边是崎岖陡峭的悬崖，体验恍若置身人间仙境的奇妙。

        圣戴维斯 (St Davids) - 英国最小的城市，人口仅有1,841，城市中却又一座宏伟壮丽的大教堂，在宗教上有重要地位。

        库姆堡 (Castle Combe) - 不要被这个名字骗了，这其实是一个充满温馨的小屋和涓涓细流的童话小镇，还被评为世界十个最美小镇之一。


        您的旅程


        威尔士有些奇怪。
        不知道为什么，这个小小的国家每平方英里拥有的城堡要比欧洲其他任何国家多上许多。更令人难以置信的是，这里20%的领土都是受保护的国家公园。就连他们的国旗上都有一条奇怪的龙，所以米字旗都不方便加上去。
        但你知道最奇怪的是什么吗？如果您有幸游玩威尔士，您会经历到许多令人难以置信的冒险和来自当地人民热情的欢迎。

        行程

        第一天

        • 寻觅居住在埃文河畔斯特拉特福的都铎王朝
        • 威尔士边界绵延不绝的山丘上的奇妙冒险


        清晨您将离开伦敦，前往威廉·莎士比亚的出生地和故乡，埃文河畔斯特拉特福。

        小镇是一座历史悠久的集镇，保留了不少精美的历史建筑，围环绕着埃文河畔的田园风光，小镇上美丽的黑白木框架建筑从都铎时代以来就几乎没有变化，洋溢着浓郁的都铎王朝时代风情，走进小镇，会不由得觉得自己好像穿越了。

        你在威尔士的第一站是海依(Hay on Wye)，“书之城”(town of books)，就坐落在布雷肯比肯国家公园(Brecon beacon National Park)的北面。

        一路行驶会到达壮丽的寒武纪山脉和美丽的艾伦谷的中心。 下午晚些时候您将会离开这里，大约一个小时的车程之后您将抵达到美丽的海滨小镇和文化中心阿伯里斯特威斯 (Aberystwyth)，接下来的两个晚上您将停留在这里。

        请注意:在某些日期，这个旅程的行程是反过来的。


        第二天

        • 欣赏斯诺登尼亚国家公园(Snowdonia National Park)的陡峭险峻纵横交错的群山峻岭
        • 对比威尔士两座宏伟的中世纪城堡，分别位于哈莱克(Harlech)和克里基厄斯(Criccieth)


        今天您将一路向北到达斯诺登尼亚国家公园(Snowdonia National Park)
        游览气势雄伟群山环抱的山区。接着您将参观园内最美丽的贝德盖勒特 (Beddgelert) 山村，在那里您可以看到著名的义犬'杰里特(Gelert)'之墓，还可以探索附近的铜矿。


        继续向波斯马多格(Porthmadog)行驶，经过Cob堤道之后， 您将到达哈莱克城堡。 这座强大的中世纪城堡坐落在高处，气势宏伟，俯瞰威尔士海岸，看起来坚不可摧。哈莱克城堡都是重要的军事据点，联合国教科文组织描述这座城堡为“欧洲13世纪晚期最好的军事建筑”。

        傍晚您将回到您在阿伯里斯特威斯的住所。

        请注意:在某些日期，这个旅程的行程是反过来的。


        第三天

        • 漫步在新码头，梦想着自己有一天能够搬到这个风景如画的小镇上
        • 在英国唯一的罗马金矿中寻找黄金


        今天您将离开阿伯里斯特威斯，穿过卡迪根湾的海岸，穿过古雅的阿贝拉尼(Aberaeron)镇到达新码头。

        豪华的沙质港口，粉刷成白色的房子，以及与威尔士诗人迪伦托马斯的密不可分的纽带：这个小镇是英国最好看和最有趣的小镇之一。更重要的是，如果幸运的话你可以在堤坝上看见海豚和鲸鱼。

        往内陆走，这里有英国唯一的罗马金矿，您可以在多勒柯提金矿 (Dolaucothi)淘黄金，说不定一不小心就发家致富了。

        在我们抵达海岸游览拉恩之前，如果在城堡下面继续向前走，运气好的话您可能有机会在发现一个神秘的岩床洞， 迪伦托马斯就是在那里写了著名的诗“牛奶树下”(Under Milk Wood)。

        接着您将到达离这里不远的滕比(Tenby)，这个风景如画，历史悠久的海滨小镇，将成为您接下来2晚入住的地方。

        请注意:在某些日期，这个旅程的行程是反过来的。


        第四天

        • 游览英国唯一的海岸国家公园，欣赏其独具特色的沿海自然风光。
        • 探索英最小的城市，圣戴维斯和其壮丽的大教堂


        您在威尔士最后一天的行程是探索最远端的西南部。

        您将在彭布罗克郡海岸国家公园见到雄壮海岸线。这个地区的景色是惊人的，这里拥有威尔士最好的海滩、海角、海湾、峭壁以及旖旎的海边城镇，还有世界最长的沿海栈道，沿着峭壁漫步，体验恍若置身人间仙境的奇妙。这里还是圣威尔士和都铎王朝的发源地。

        接着，您将前往圣戴维斯小教堂城市，在那里探索城市并享用午餐。

        下然后在彭布罗克(Pembroke)停下来，在那里你可以进入城堡厚重的中世纪城墙。

        今天您将从这里再次返回到您的住处滕比Tenby，并度过一个放松的下午。

        请注意:在某些日期，这个旅程的行程是反过来的。


        第五天

        • 参观卡利恩 (Caerleon)的国家历史博物馆和罗马城堡深入了解历史
        • 在科茨沃尔德的童话小镇库姆堡休闲漫步 (Castle Combe)


        清晨您将收拾行李，离开滕比，踏上返回伦敦的旅程。

        司机将驱车经过南威尔士的大工业城市卡迪夫的郊区，参观室外国家历史博物馆。

        接着，回到2000年前，在卡利恩(Caerleon) 保存完好的罗马城堡停下。

        从这里便是时候告别威尔士了。 我们经过赛文大桥（Severn）回到英国，您将在库姆堡 (Castle Combe)稍事休息一下。

        库姆堡镇被喻为"英格兰最漂亮的村子(The prettiest village in England)"， 据说也是英国古老街道保存最完好的一个小镇。 全镇只一条石板弧型小街，宁静可爱，格调一致的英式小屋，黑色的转瓦、淡黄色的墙壁。漫步在库姆堡街道头， 会让人产生时光倒流的错觉， 好似时间停在了五百年前。

        您的司机/导游将在傍晚带您回到伦敦市中心。

        请注意:在某些日期，这个旅程的行程是反过来的。

        住宿

        Where does this tour stay overnight?

        You stay two nights in Aberystwyth and two nights in Tenby.


        How does the accommodation work?

        Your tour excludes accommodation; but when you book, you can ask us to reserve local accommodation for you, or you can choose to reserve your own accommodation in the overnight locations.


        I want Rabbie’s to book my accommodation, what happens now?

        Once you've booked your tour, we'll reserve all your accommodation, and your driver will drop you off with your hosts. You’ll pay your accommodation hosts directly. Most accommodation hosts don’t accept credit cards, so be prepared to pay cash.

        Due to availability, we may not be able to reserve your first choice of accommodation. When this happens we’ll book your second choice, and If there are any further issues we’ll contact you.


        What if I want to change my accommodation booking with Rabbie’s?

        Changes to accommodation cannot be guaranteed and are subject to availability. A minimum amendment charge of £5.00 will apply, increasing to a maximum of £25.00 depending on the length of your tour.


        I booked my own accommodation is there anything I need to do?

        You’ll need to contact us with your accommodation details. Please only reserve accommodation within the overnight towns that your tour stops in. And if you’re unsure, please check with us first.


        Your driver will drop you off at your chosen accommodation as long as it’s relatively central.


        What if I cancel my tour at short notice?

        If you cancel less than three days before departure, you may be liable for the cost of your first nights’ accommodation. And occasionally you may be liable for all your accommodation costs.


        What are the accommodation types and what do they cost?

        We try to reserve accommodation that’s no more than a 20-minute walk from the town centre. But during busy periods there can be a walk of up to 30 minutes.


        We regularly check our accommodation suppliers to ensure they reach a certain standard. The prices below are for guidance only.


        B&B (Bed and Breakfast) Standard

        The toilet and showering facilities are shared with at least one other rooms in the house, and the price includes breakfast. The cost is between £30.00 - £35.00 per person per night. A single standard is between £35.00 - £60.00 per night.



        B&B En/suite

        The showering and toilet facilities are private, and breakfast is included in the price. The expected cost for a room, based on two people sharing, will be between £40.00 – £50.00 per person per night.

        The expected cost for a single room will be between *£55.00 - £65.00 per night. If you travel during a busy period, you can expect to pay up to *£75 - £80 per night. Rooms with a view are limited and may be more expensive.

        In Ireland B&B's will be around €45 - €55 per person per night.


        Hotels

        Hotels have a bar or restaurant and provide evening meals. Breakfast is included in the price. The expected cost of Hotel 3* is between £65.00 - £85.00 per person per night. Hotel 4* is approximately £75.00 - £120.00 per person per night. Please be advised that 4* hotels aren’t available in some smaller rural areas of the Scottish Highlands and islands.

        In Ireland, Hotels are around €55 - €65 per person per night.


        Hostels

        A hostel consists of dormitory rooms with 4 to 16 bunk beds. Towels aren’t provided, but you can hire towels there. The bathroom facilities are a short distance from the dorm room. Kitchen facilities are equipped so you can make your own meals. The cost is between £15.00- £25.00 per person per night.

        Please note that SYHA hostels only offer same gender dorms. Some independent hostels will offer mixed rooms (males and females together). If you have a preference, please let us know at the time of booking.

        常见问题

        Do you offer hotel pickups in the departure cities?

        The UK and Ireland's historic city centres are full of narrow streets and winding roads. So, to speed up your departure, we only pick up from our central meeting points. What's more is leaving the city using the most direct route also reduces the carbon footprint of our tours.

        Is there parking close to the departure point?

        We don't recommend bringing your car into the centre of our departure cities. It can be hard to find parking, and there are excellent public transportation services.

        When should I get to the departure point?

        Arrive 15 minutes before your departure time. Due to waiting restrictions, we can't wait for you if you're late.

        What happens if I'm late or miss the tour?

        If you're late for your tour, our cancellation policy applies.

        What are your cancellation policies?

        We have a cancellation period of 48 hours for our one day tours, 15 days for two to nine day tours, and 21 days for 10+ day tours. If cancelled outside this period, we'll give you a full refund minus a 10% admin charge. If you cancel inside this period, refunds are at our discretion.         Can Itineraries change?

        Your tour will always follow the itinerary as closely as possible. While there are ongoing coronavirus restrictions throughout the UK and Ireland, itineraries are more likely to change than usual as opening hours and capacities at many visitor attractions are reduced and subject to change. Your driver-guide will keep you up to up to date on the road, and we'll try to get in touch about any large changes prior to your departure.

        What is a guaranteed departure?

        It's simple: you book, you go. This means we'll never cancel your tour because of a minimum numbers policy.

        Can I pre-book a specific seat?

        Due to logistical reasons, we're unable to let you pre-book a seat on the bus. But our comfortable 16-seat mini-coaches have huge windows. So, you'll always get a good view.

        Are there toilets on the bus?

        Unfortunately, we have no on-board toilet facilities, but you'll have regular comfort breaks.

        How do you cater for people with accessibility needs?

        We aim to accommodate everyone. To read more information on how we do this, click here to go to our accessibility guide.

        Do you take children on your tours?

        We regret we don't carry children under the age of five on our scheduled tours.

        You can ask us about the tours we recommend for younger children. And if your child is less than 1.35m tall, you should let us know, so we can provide them with a suitable booster seat.

        How much time do I get off the bus?

        We want you to enjoy Britain and Ireland. And we believe the best way to do this is to get you off the mini-coach and out exploring. So, we give you plenty of time to take photos, go walking, and explore ancient ruins.

        Why do you exclude visitor attractions?

        We exclude visitor attractions in your tour price because it lets you travel your way. It means you can pamper yourself with a luxury lunch or skip an attraction whenever you feel like it.

        Do we need to bring our own lunch?

        You don't need to. We stop in small towns, villages, and rural eateries where you can choose whatever meal pleases both your taste buds and your budget.

        Should I tip the driver-guide?

        Let's be honest, our driver-guides would never refuse a cheeky bonus. But it's up to you, and the amount is entirely at your discretion.

        What about travel insurance?

        We advise you take out travel insurance.

        What is ABTOT?

        You're protected by ABTOT when you chose to let us reserve your accommodation on a multi-day tour. This means you can claim back anything you've paid us if we become bankrupt. Read our terms and conditions for more information.
